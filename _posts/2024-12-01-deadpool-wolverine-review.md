Ryan Reynolds and Hugh Jackman. Together. Finally.

Deadpool & Wolverine is the shot in the arm the MCU desperately needed. It's loud, it's crude, it's self-aware to a fault — and it works almost every time it tries.

## What Works

The chemistry between Reynolds and Jackman is undeniable. Their banter feels genuine, probably because Reynolds has been pushing for this film for years and it shows. Wolverine's arc — a Logan who failed his world — gives the film actual emotional weight under all the fourth-wall-breaking chaos.

The action sequences are spectacular, especially the final act set piece. The multiversal cameos land better than expected, and the film earns its emotional beats without being manipulative about it.

## What Doesn't

The first act drags before the two leads get together. Some of the meta-jokes overstay their welcome, and the villain, while serviceable, doesn't hit the heights of the best MCU antagonists.

## Verdict

If you've been waiting for Marvel to find its footing again, Deadpool & Wolverine is a very good sign. It proves the studio can still make crowd-pleasing, genuinely funny blockbusters when it gets out of its own way.
